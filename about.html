About SyncDash

SyncDash is a self-initiated portfolio project that simulates the internal tooling a Technical Account Manager uses every day — account health monitoring, QBR workflows, renewal tracking, and AI-assisted meeting prep.

It is designed to look, feel, and function like a real CS operations platform — because the thinking behind it is real.

Project purpose

Why this tool was built

SyncDash exists to answer a question every TAM or CSM hiring manager has when reviewing a resume: does this person actually understand how account management works at the operational level?

Rather than describe the work, SyncDash demonstrates it. The platform shows how a TAM thinks about account health, adoption signals, renewal risk, and client communication — built into a working tool with real data structures and real AI integration.

  • Model how health scores, adoption trends, and renewal risk are tracked across a book of business.
  • Show what a pre-call QBR workflow looks like from internal prep to client-facing delivery.
  • Demonstrate how AI can assist with account prep without replacing the human judgment that matters most.
  • Illustrate the difference between an internal team view and a client-facing conversation.
What the project highlights

Skills and focus areas

SyncDash is built to surface the capabilities most relevant to Technical Account Management, Customer Success, and Implementation roles.

  • Translating account signals (adoption, NPS, tickets) into actionable account strategy.
  • Structuring QBR workflows that serve both internal alignment and client-facing meetings.
  • Using AI to prepare for hard conversations — not avoid them.
  • Understanding what data matters to a TAM vs. what matters to a client.
  • Building tools that reflect how CS operations actually work, not how they look on slides.

How recruiters and hiring managers can read this project
  • TAM operations lens: How account health, adoption, and renewal risk are surfaced and acted on across a portfolio.
  • QBR workflow lens: How an internal prep meeting translates into a client-facing conversation — with different data, tone, and framing for each.
  • AI integration lens: How Sage (powered by Anthropic) assists with meeting prep, discussion prompts, and notes generation without replacing the TAM's judgment.
  • Technical lens: How a non-engineer built a working, data-driven application using front-end tooling, external APIs, and a real database backend.
In short: SyncDash is a working TAM operations tool built around fictional data. It is intentionally designed to reflect how CS and TAM work actually happens — account reviews, client prep, internal alignment, renewal conversations — while making clear here that it was conceived, designed, and built by one person as a demonstration of how they think about accounts, tools, and AI-assisted workflows.
Companion project

SyncDash + SyncToScale — the full picture

SyncDash does not exist in isolation. It is the TAM-side tool built on top of SyncToScale — a fictional B2B automation platform that serves as the product SyncDash customers use.

Together they tell a complete story: SyncToScale is the product. SyncDash is what the account team sees when they manage it. One is the vendor platform. The other is the operational layer on top of it. The same accounts, the same data, two completely different views.

This pairing demonstrates an understanding of both sides of a SaaS relationship — the product experience a customer has, and the operational reality a TAM manages every day.

SyncDash is a personal project built by Christy McSwain to demonstrate TAM and CS operations thinking, AI-assisted workflows, and front-end development capability.
